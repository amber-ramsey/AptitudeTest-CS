As mentioned before, this test is for practice only, so you should not regard your result as of importance chiefly because the test was not taken under proper test conditions e.g. in a silent room with no disturbances. Your performance can also be distorted if you have a cold, or have not slept well. Also, evidence shows that international students or those from ethnic minorities may be disadvantaged in this type of test, due to language and cultural differences. If your first language is not English, your score is likely to be lower on such tests than native English speakers. Mature students may also sometimes be disadvantaged and you should remember that your degree subject may change your performance — for example scientists are likely to do better on mathematical tests and humanities students on verbal tests. Even your ability to use a mouse may influence your score! Having said all this it is still natural for you to want some feedback on your performance.

22 58% In a chocolate factory, a machine takes a 1 kg block of chocolate. It then divides this into rectangles each weighing 10g. These rectangles are then stamped into disks of chocolate each weighing 6g with the remaining chocolate discarded. These chocolate disks are then packed into bags of 4 which are sealed and finally packed into boxes, each containing 6 bags ready for dispatch to the shops. What weight of chocolate out of the original kilogram will be contained in COMPLETELY FILLED boxes (i.e. those containing a full 6 bags)?

1000 g of chocolate is made into 100 rectangles of chocolate each weighting 10g.
These are then stamped into 100 disks of 6g each.
4 disks are packed into each bag and then 6 bags are placed into each box.
Therefore there are 24 disks in each box.
As there are 100 disks in total produced from the 1 Kg of chocolate, there will therefore be four boxes with 4 disks left over.
These 4 boxes contain 96 disks each weighing 6g.
So the total weight of chocolate in the boxes will be 96 × 6g = 576g. 
Converting to a percentage: 576/1000 × 100 = 57.6% which rounds to 58%

It is 4 o’clock. What is the measure of the angle formed between the hour hand and the minute hand?

At 3 o'clock, the angle between the hour and minute hands is 90 degrees.
To change from 3 to 4 o'clock you must find out the angle between 2 hours.
The angle between 12 and 3 can be divided into 3 sections to get the angle of a single hour.
90 degrees ÷ 3 sections = 30 degrees between each hour.
Since we know the distance from one hour to the next is 30 degrees, we can add 30 degrees to the 90 degrees to get from 3 o'clock to 4 o'clock.
30 degrees + 90 degrees = 120 degrees
What is the measure of the angle formed between the hour hand and the minute hand? 120 degrees

30 If an animal is not in group B, it is not in group A.

If an animal is in group A, they are also in group B.
If the above statement is true, which of the following statements must also be true?

A way to simplify what is said above is, if p (an animal is in group A), then q (the animal is in group B).
If an animal is in group B, it is also in group A. <- This statement is if q then p, which is not equivallent.
If an animal is not in group B, it is not in group A. <- This statement says if not q then not p. Since the statement above states that the animals in group A will be in group B, if the animal is not in B it can not be in A. This is the equivallent statement.
If an animal is not in group A, it is not in group B. <- This sentence is if not p then not q, however the animal can be in group B without being in group A.
If an animal is in group B, it is not in group A. <- This statement says if q then not p, which is not equivallent.
If an animal is not in group B, it is in group A. <- This sentence says if not q then p, however if p is true the q must also be true, therefore it is not equivallent.
Which of the statements must also be true? If an animal is not in group B, it is not in group A.
