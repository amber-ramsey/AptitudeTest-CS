Title of Research: Predictors to Success in Introductory Computer Science Programming Classes

Funding Agency/Sponsor: N/A

What is the purpose of the research?
This study's research objective is to determine the factors through testing that show or suggest an expected increase in success within Computer Science programming classes.

For how long am I expected to be in this study and how much of my time is required?
The study will take no more than 30 minutes to complete.

Where is this study to take place?
The study will take place wherever you are comfortable provided you have access to a computer.

How many participants will be in this study?
As many as 20-50 people will participate in this study.

What is my involvement for participating in this study?
Your involvement in this study will be logging into the site provided by your test administrator and taking the aptitude test.

What are the risks and/or potential discomforts of participating in this study?
There are no serious risks associated with this study. Discomfort or inconveniences may arise with prolonged use of the computer or laptop; however, this is not expected. If at any time you experience any level of discomfort from using your computer, please notify an investigator.

What are the anticipated benefits of participating in this study?
By participating in this study, you will gain first-hand experience with the types of skills that are common within Computer Science programming classes.

Will I be compensated for participating in this study?
If your instructor offers extra credit for participation, your professor will receive an email stating you have completed the study and are eligible for extra credit. No other compensation will be provided.

What is an alternative procedure(s) that I can choose instead of participating in this study?
There is no alternative procedure for participating in this study.

How will my confidentiality be protected?
Your name must be provided for verification purposes only. The information you provide through questionnaires and other data obtained during the evaluation will be fully anonymous and stored digitally. Data, along with consent forms, will be kept secure via password protection. Only the investigators will have access to participants’ data. There are no study-specific issues that may result in an increased risk of breach of confidentiality.

Is my participation voluntary?
Yes, your participation in this research study is voluntary.

Can I stop participating at any time during this research without penalty?
Yes, you are also free to withdraw from this study at any time.

How can I withdraw from this study if I choose not to participate any longer?
You may leave at any time by indicating to the evaluator that you are withdrawing.

Will I receive a copy of this informed consent document?
Informed consent MUST be documented by the use of a written consent form approved by the IRB, and signed by you or your legally authorized representative. A waiver of this requirement can only be granted by CSU’s Campus Institutional Review Board for the Protection of Human Subjects in Research, in accordance with 45 CFR 46. Upon request, a copy of this form will be given to you for your records. You must be over 18 to give your informed consent and participate.

Who should I contact if I have questions regarding this study?
Dr. Sean T. Hayes, Department of Computer Science, 843-863-7369, shayes@csuniv.edu

By printing your name below, you indicate that you have read and fully understand the information above, you have freely decided to participate in this research, and you certify that you are at least 18 years old.
